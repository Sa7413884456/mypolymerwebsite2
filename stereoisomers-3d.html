Understanding PMMA Stereoisomers

🔷 Isotactic PMMA

Definition: All methyl ester groups (-COOCH₃) and methyl groups (-CH₃) are on the same side of the polymer backbone.

Tacticity: Regular (R,R,R...)
Crystallinity: Semi-crystalline
Density: 1.23-1.26 g/cm³
Tg (°C): ~120
Melting Point: ~200°C
Hardness: High
Applications: High-end optical, injection molding

🟢 Atactic PMMA

Definition: Methyl ester and methyl groups are randomly distributed on both sides of the polymer backbone.

Tacticity: Random (R,S,R,S...)
Crystallinity: Amorphous
Density: 1.18-1.20 g/cm³
Tg (°C): ~105
Melting Point: ~160°C
Hardness: Medium
Applications: Most common, general purpose acrylic

🔶 Syndiotactic PMMA

Definition: Methyl ester and methyl groups alternate regularly on opposite sides of the polymer backbone.

Tacticity: Alternating (R,S,R,S...)
Crystallinity: Semi-crystalline
Density: 1.24-1.27 g/cm³
Tg (°C): ~115
Melting Point: ~195°C
Hardness: High
Applications: Specialty polymers, high-performance
